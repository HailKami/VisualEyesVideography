How Much Does a Wedding Videographer Cost in St. Thomas?

By Jordan Petty | May 26, 2026 | St. Thomas, Virgin Islands

If you are planning a destination wedding in St. Thomas, one of the first budget questions is usually: how much does wedding videography cost? As a local videographer with 13+ years filming weddings across the Virgin Islands, I will break down realistic pricing, what drives the numbers up or down, and how to choose the right package without overpaying or under-booking.

Average Wedding Videography Cost in St. Thomas

In the USVI, most professional wedding videography packages fall into these ranges:

  • Essential coverage: roughly $2,500 – $4,000
  • Signature / full-day coverage: roughly $4,500 – $7,500
  • Premium / luxury production: $8,000+

Destination weddings at resort venues like The Westin St. Thomas, The Ritz-Carlton, or Marriott's Frenchman's Cove often land in the mid-to-premium range because couples want longer coverage, stronger audio, and cinematic deliverables they can share with family back home.

What Affects Wedding Video Pricing?

1. Hours of Coverage

Half-day coverage (ceremony + portraits) costs less than full-day coverage (getting ready through reception). Most St. Thomas weddings need at least 6–8 hours to capture the full story without rushing.

2. Number of Videographers

A second videographer adds $500 – $1,500+ depending on hours. It is worth it for larger weddings, long aisles, or when you want simultaneous coverage of both partners getting ready.

3. Deliverables

Pricing changes based on what you receive:

  • Highlight film (3–7 minutes)
  • Full ceremony film with clean audio
  • Reception speeches and toasts
  • Social media teaser
  • Documentary-style long edit

More deliverables means more editing time, which affects your final price. See our guide on what is included in wedding videography packages.

4. Drone & Aerial Footage

Drone adds cinematic island views and is popular at beach and resort venues. Expect an add-on of $300 – $800 depending on location, permits, and flight time.

5. Venue & Logistics

Beach ceremonies, ferry hops to St. John, or multi-location timelines can affect pricing. Local videographers often save money here because there is no travel markup from mainland crews flying in with cases of gear.

6. Editing & Turnaround

Rush delivery (48-hour teaser, 2-week full film) usually costs extra. Standard turnaround is often 6–12 weeks for a fully edited wedding film.

Mainland Videographer vs Local St. Thomas Videographer

Some couples import a videographer from the states. That can work, but you may pay for flights, lodging, gear shipping, and travel days on top of the production fee. A St. Thomas-based team already knows the venues, weather patterns, wind on the beach, and how to coordinate with island planners.

That local knowledge shows up in your film — and often in your total cost.

How to Budget Smart

  1. Decide your must-have moments (ceremony audio, first dance, speeches, sunset portraits)
  2. Match coverage hours to your timeline — do not book 4 hours for a 10-hour wedding day
  3. Compare packages apples-to-apples (hours, shooters, deliverables, drone)
  4. Book early — peak season (December–April) fills fast in the Virgin Islands
  5. Ask about retainers and payment plans
