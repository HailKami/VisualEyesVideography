Top Instagrammable Spots in St. Thomas for Wedding Photos

By Jordan Petty | January 27, 2025 | St. Thomas, Virgin Islands

St. Thomas, Virgin Islands is a photographer's paradise, offering countless stunning backdrops for wedding photos. As a professional videographer and photographer who has worked throughout the island for over 13 years, I've discovered the most photogenic locations that will make your wedding photos absolutely stunning. Here are the top Instagrammable spots in St. Thomas that will give you picture-perfect memories.

1. Magens Bay Beach

Why It's Perfect:

Consistently ranked among the world's most beautiful beaches, Magens Bay offers pristine white sand, crystal-clear turquoise water, and lush green hillsides. The bay's unique horseshoe shape creates a stunning natural frame for photos.

Best Times: Early morning (7-9 AM) for soft golden light, or late afternoon (4-6 PM) for dramatic sunset shots

Photo Opportunities: Beach portraits, water shots, palm tree silhouettes, sunset ceremonies

2. Drake's Seat Overlook

Why It's Perfect:

This elevated overlook offers panoramic views of Magens Bay and the surrounding islands. It's one of the most photographed spots on St. Thomas, providing breathtaking backdrop for wedding photos.

Best Times: Morning for clear views, sunset for romantic golden hour lighting

Photo Opportunities: Dramatic landscape shots, couple silhouettes against the view, engagement-style portraits

3. Charlotte Amalie Historic District

Why It's Perfect:

The historic Danish colonial architecture, colorful buildings, and cobblestone streets create a unique, European-inspired backdrop. Perfect for couples who want something different from typical beach photos.

Best Times: Morning or late afternoon to avoid harsh midday sun

Photo Opportunities: Architectural details, colorful doorways, historic streets, unique cultural shots

4. Mountain Top

Why It's Perfect:

At 1,500 feet above sea level, Mountain Top offers 360-degree views of the entire island and surrounding Caribbean Sea. The elevated perspective creates dramatic, unique wedding photos.

Best Times: Clear days, morning or afternoon

Photo Opportunities: Aerial-style shots, dramatic landscapes, couple portraits with panoramic views

5. Secret Harbour Beach

Why It's Perfect:

More secluded than Magens Bay, Secret Harbour offers intimate beach settings with beautiful water and fewer crowds. Perfect for romantic, private-feeling photos.

Best Times: Early morning for privacy and soft light

Photo Opportunities: Intimate beach shots, water portraits, secluded ceremony locations

6. Coki Beach

Why It's Perfect:

Known for its vibrant coral reef and clear waters, Coki Beach offers unique underwater and above-water photo opportunities. The colorful marine life creates interesting backgrounds.

Best Times: Morning for clearest water visibility

Photo Opportunities: Beach shots, water activities, colorful tropical backgrounds

7. Bluebeard's Castle

Why It's Perfect:

This historic site offers dramatic architecture, stone walls, and elevated views of Charlotte Amalie harbor. Perfect for couples who want a mix of history and romance.

Best Times: Late afternoon for golden hour lighting

Photo Opportunities: Historic architecture, dramatic stone backdrops, harbor views

8. Lindquist Beach (Smith Bay Park)

Why It's Perfect:

One of St. Thomas's most pristine beaches, Lindquist offers untouched natural beauty with fewer crowds. The protected park setting ensures beautiful, natural backdrops.

Best Times: Early morning or late afternoon

Photo Opportunities: Natural beach settings, tropical vegetation, pristine water shots

9. Frenchman's Bay

Why It's Perfect:

This secluded bay offers calm waters and beautiful natural surroundings. Less crowded than popular beaches, it provides intimate photo opportunities.

Best Times: Morning for calmest conditions

Photo Opportunities: Intimate beach shots, calm water reflections, natural settings

10. Paradise Point

Why It's Perfect:

Accessible by tram, Paradise Point offers stunning views of Charlotte Amalie and the harbor. The elevated location provides unique perspectives for wedding photos.

Best Times: Sunset for dramatic lighting and city lights

Photo Opportunities: Cityscape backgrounds, sunset shots, elevated perspectives

Tips for Perfect Wedding Photos in St. Thomas

Timing is Everything

  • Golden Hour: The hour after sunrise and before sunset provides the most flattering light
  • Avoid Midday: Harsh overhead sun creates unflattering shadows
  • Weather Awareness: Brief tropical showers can create beautiful dramatic skies

What to Bring

  • Backup outfits (beach photos can get wet!)
  • Comfortable shoes for walking between locations
  • Touch-up makeup and hair products
  • Water and snacks for longer photo sessions
  • Props that reflect your personality

Working with Your Photographer

  • Discuss your vision and must-have shots beforehand
  • Trust your photographer's local knowledge
  • Be flexible—weather and lighting conditions can change quickly
  • Plan for travel time between locations

Creating Your Photo Timeline

When planning your wedding day photography, consider:

  • Getting Ready: Capture the anticipation and preparation
  • First Look: Private moment before the ceremony
  • Ceremony: The main event at your chosen venue
  • Portrait Session: Dedicated time for couple and group photos
  • Reception: Candid moments and celebrations

Many couples choose to do their portrait session at a different location from their ceremony, allowing them to capture multiple stunning backdrops throughout the day.

Making It Personal

While these locations are stunning, the best wedding photos tell your unique story. Work with your photographer to incorporate locations that have meaning to you, or discover hidden gems that reflect your personality.

As a local St. Thomas photographer, I know not just the popular spots, but also the hidden locations that offer unique perspectives. Whether you want classic beach shots, dramatic overlooks, or historic architecture, St. Thomas has it all.
