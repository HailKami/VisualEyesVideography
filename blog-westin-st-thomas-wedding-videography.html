Wedding Videography at The Westin St. Thomas: What Couples Should Know

By Jordan Petty | May 22, 2026 | St. Thomas, Virgin Islands

The Westin St. Thomas Beach Resort & Spa at Frenchman's Reef is one of the most sought-after destination wedding venues in the Virgin Islands. With oceanfront ceremony spaces, massive outdoor terraces, and resort-level coordination, it is built for cinematic wedding films. I have filmed a wedding at The Westin St. Thomas firsthand — here is what couples and planners should know when booking videography for this property.

Why The Westin St. Thomas Is Perfect on Video

  • Panoramic ocean views from cliffside and terrace locations
  • Multiple ceremony backdrops — gazebo, beach, pool deck, and ballroom options
  • 85,000+ sq. ft. of event space for large guest counts and grand entrances
  • Resort infrastructure — power, lighting, and indoor backup for weather
  • No passport required for U.S. guests — ideal for destination wedding video sharing with family at home

Best Filming Locations on Property

Oceanfront Gazebo

The gazebo is a signature Westin ceremony spot. It delivers wide ocean horizons and dramatic wide shots. For video, schedule your ceremony in the late afternoon when the light is softer and the water glows behind the altar. Wind can pick up — your videographer should use lav mics and a backup audio recorder on the officiant and groom.

Seacliff Terrace & Harbor View Deck

These spaces are ideal for cocktail hour B-roll, golden-hour portraits, and drone establishing shots that show the scale of the resort against the Caribbean. They also work beautifully for first look reveals with a cinematic walk-in.

Beach & Pool Areas

Morningstar Beach vibes and poolside celebrations give your film energy and color. Midday ceremonies here can be bright — an experienced local videographer will expose for skin tones and use ND filters so footage stays filmic, not blown out.

Harbor View Ballroom

For receptions and rain backup, the ballroom gives controlled lighting and clean speech audio. Rehearsal dinner coverage here also adds a nice chapter to your wedding story.

Sample Wedding Day Timeline (Video-Friendly)

  1. Getting ready — 1.5–2 hours (room prep, details, first look optional)
  2. Ceremony — 30–45 minutes plus 15 minutes for guest congratulations
  3. Golden hour portraits — 30–45 minutes at terrace or beach
  4. Reception — grand entrance through last dance (3–4 hours minimum)

Most Westin weddings need 8–10 hours of videography coverage to capture the full story without gaps. Read more in our packages guide.

Audio, Wind & Weather Tips

Outdoor resort weddings look incredible but come with trade-offs:

  • Coastal wind affects ceremony audio — use wireless lavaliers, not just camera mics
  • Have a rain plan confirmed with the planner 48 hours out
  • Keep a 15-minute buffer between ceremony and portraits for lighting shifts at sunset
  • Brief the DJ on mic handoff for toasts so speeches are clean in your final film

Why Hire a Local Videographer Who Knows The Westin

Resort weddings move fast. A St. Thomas videographer who knows The Westin's layout can pre-scout angles, coordinate with security and staff, and position cameras without blocking guest views. That means fewer missed moments and a smoother day for your planner.

Importing a mainland crew adds travel costs and learning curve on property. Local teams arrive with backup gear, know the ferry and traffic patterns, and can return for reshoots if weather forces a schedule change.
