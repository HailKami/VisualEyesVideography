Destination Wedding Planning Guide: St. Thomas, Virgin Islands

By Jordan Petty | April 10, 2025 | St. Thomas, Virgin Islands

Planning a destination wedding in St. Thomas, Virgin Islands is an exciting adventure that combines tropical paradise with unforgettable memories. As a local videographer who has worked with countless couples planning their perfect day, I've seen what makes St. Thomas weddings successful. This comprehensive guide will help you navigate the planning process and create the wedding of your dreams.

Why Choose St. Thomas for Your Destination Wedding?

St. Thomas offers everything you could want for a destination wedding:

  • Stunning Natural Beauty: Pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush tropical landscapes
  • Easy Access: Direct flights from major US cities
  • US Territory: No passport required for US citizens, US currency, English language
  • Year-Round Weather: Tropical climate perfect for outdoor ceremonies
  • Diverse Venues: From intimate beach settings to luxury resorts

Timeline: When to Start Planning

12-18 Months Before

  • Choose your date and venue
  • Book major vendors (photographer, videographer, coordinator)
  • Send save-the-dates
  • Research and book accommodations

6-12 Months Before

  • Apply for marriage license and permits
  • Book remaining vendors (florist, DJ, caterer)
  • Plan guest accommodations and group rates
  • Create wedding website with travel information

3-6 Months Before

  • Send formal invitations
  • Finalize menu and details with venue
  • Plan welcome events and activities
  • Arrange transportation for guests

1-3 Months Before

  • Finalize guest count
  • Confirm all vendor bookings
  • Plan rehearsal dinner
  • Create day-of timeline

Marriage License and Legal Requirements

Getting married in the US Virgin Islands requires a marriage license. Here's what you need to know:

Marriage License Requirements:

  • Both parties must appear in person at the Territorial Court
  • Valid government-issued photo ID
  • Social Security numbers
  • If previously married, divorce decree or death certificate
  • Application fee (check current rates)
  • 8-day waiting period after application

Important: The 8-day waiting period means you must apply at least 8 days before your wedding date. Many couples apply during a pre-wedding visit or arrive early to handle this requirement.

Beach Wedding Permits

If you're planning a beach ceremony, you'll need a permit from the Department of Planning and Natural Resources (DPNR).

  • Application Time: Apply at least 30 days in advance
  • Fees: Vary by location and group size
  • Restrictions: Some beaches have size limits or time restrictions
  • Popular Beach Locations: Magens Bay, Coki Beach, Secret Harbour

Your wedding coordinator or venue can often help with the permit process, but it's important to understand the requirements early in your planning.

Best Time of Year for St. Thomas Weddings

Peak Season (December - April)

  • Weather: Dry season with lower humidity
  • Pros: Most predictable weather, comfortable temperatures
  • Cons: Higher prices, more crowds, venues book faster

Shoulder Season (May - June, November)

  • Weather: Generally good with occasional brief showers
  • Pros: Better availability, moderate pricing
  • Cons: Slightly less predictable weather

Off Season (July - October)

  • Weather: Hotter, more humid, hurricane season
  • Pros: Lower prices, better availability
  • Cons: Hurricane risk, higher humidity

My Recommendation: December through April offers the most reliable weather, but May and November can be excellent choices with better availability and pricing.

Choosing Your Venue

St. Thomas offers diverse wedding venues:

  • Beach Venues: Magens Bay, Coki Beach, Secret Harbour
  • Resort Venues: The Ritz-Carlton, Frenchman's Reef, Bolongo Bay
  • Private Estates: Luxury villas with ocean views
  • Historic Locations: Fort Christian, Bluebeard's Castle

Consider your guest count, style preference, and budget when choosing. Many venues offer all-inclusive packages that simplify planning.

Working with Local Vendors

Working with St. Thomas-based vendors offers significant advantages:

Benefits of Local Vendors:

  • Knowledge of local conditions and regulations
  • Established relationships with venues
  • No travel fees or accommodation costs
  • Understanding of island logistics
  • Ability to adapt quickly to changes

Guest Considerations

Make your guests' experience smooth and enjoyable:

  • Travel Information: Provide clear details on flights, airports, and transportation
  • Accommodations: Negotiate group rates at hotels or recommend vacation rentals
  • Welcome Events: Plan a welcome dinner or cocktail hour
  • Activities: Suggest local attractions and activities
  • Timeline: Give guests plenty of notice (6-12 months)

Weather Backup Plans

Even in paradise, weather can be unpredictable. Always have a backup plan:

  • Choose venues with indoor options
  • Consider tent rentals for outdoor venues
  • Have a clear rain plan communicated to all vendors
  • Trust your local vendors' weather knowledge

Budget Considerations

Destination weddings can be cost-effective, but consider:

  • Travel Costs: Flights and accommodations for you and guests
  • Vendor Costs: Some vendors may charge travel fees
  • Permits and Fees: Marriage license, beach permits, venue fees
  • Guest Experience: Welcome events, transportation, activities

Local Tips from a St. Thomas Videographer

Insider Advice:

  • Book vendors early, especially during peak season
  • Consider weekday weddings for better availability and pricing
  • Plan for island time—things move at a relaxed pace
  • Have a local wedding coordinator—they're invaluable
  • Trust your vendors' local knowledge
  • Plan for extra time—everything takes longer on island

Making It Personal

Your St. Thomas wedding should reflect your unique style while embracing the island's natural beauty. Whether you want a barefoot beach ceremony or an elegant resort celebration, the Virgin Islands offers the perfect backdrop.
