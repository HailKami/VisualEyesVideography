What's Included in Wedding Videography Packages? (St. Thomas Guide)

By Jordan Petty | May 19, 2026 | St. Thomas, Virgin Islands

Not all wedding video packages are built the same. One quote might include a cinematic highlight and clean ceremony audio; another might only give you raw files and a short clip. If you are planning a St. Thomas or USVI destination wedding, here is exactly what to look for — and what VisualEyes Videography includes at each level.

The Core Parts of Every Wedding Video Package

1. Coverage Hours

This is the foundation. Coverage hours define when your videographer arrives and leaves. Typical tiers:

  • Essential (6 hours): Ceremony, portraits, and key reception moments
  • Signature (8 hours): Getting ready through first dances and speeches
  • Premium (10+ hours): Full-day storytelling including prep, ceremony, reception, and exit

At resort venues like The Westin St. Thomas, 8 hours is usually the minimum for a complete film.

2. Highlight Film

Your highlight is the cinematic 3–7 minute film you share on social media and show family. It should include the best visuals, vows (if permitted), laughter, dancing, and island scenery. This is the piece most couples rewatch the most.

3. Ceremony Film

A dedicated edit of the full ceremony with:

  • Clear vow audio
  • Stable multicam angles when available
  • Processional and recessional intact

Beach ceremonies in St. Thomas need strong audio planning because of wind — confirm your package includes ceremony audio capture, not just pretty visuals.

4. Reception Coverage

Reception coverage typically includes grand entrance, first dance, parent dances, cake cutting, and speeches. Ask whether your package caps speech recording at a certain number or includes all toasts.

5. Delivery Format & Timeline

Confirm how you receive files (online gallery, download link, USB) and standard turnaround (often 8–12 weeks). Premium packages may include a 48-hour teaser or social media cut.

Common Add-Ons

  • Second videographer — simultaneous coverage of both partners and wider ceremony angles
  • Drone / aerial footage — popular for resort and beach weddings in the Virgin Islands
  • Rehearsal dinner coverage — great for destination weekends
  • Extended highlight or documentary edit — 15–20 minute long-form story
  • Rush editing — faster delivery for a fee
  • Raw footage — sometimes included, sometimes an add-on

What Is Usually NOT Included

Read contracts carefully. These items are often extra or excluded:

  • Travel fees for videographers based off-island (local teams often have none)
  • Permits for drone or park/beach locations
  • Unlimited revision rounds
  • Same-day edit at reception unless explicitly listed
  • Copyright buyout for commercial use — personal use is standard

How to Compare Packages Apples to Apples

  1. Match total coverage hours
  2. Count videographers on site
  3. List every deliverable by name (highlight, ceremony, speeches, teaser)
  4. Ask about audio strategy for outdoor ceremonies
  5. Confirm backup gear and rain plan
  6. Check retainer amount and payment schedule
